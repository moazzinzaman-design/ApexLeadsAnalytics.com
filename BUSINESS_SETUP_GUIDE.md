# Apex Leads Analytics - Business Setup Guide

A step-by-step guide to setting up Apex Leads Analytics as a legitimate business in the UK.

---

## Business Structure Options

### Option 1: Sole Trader (Easiest - Recommended for Starting)
**Cost**: £0-50 | **Time**: 1 day

**Pros**:
- Easiest to set up
- Complete control
- Simple tax returns
- No registration fee

**Cons**:
- Unlimited personal liability
- May be harder to raise investment

**How to Register**:
1. Go to HMRC website
2. Register for Self Assessment
3. Get a National Insurance number (if you don't have one)

**Tax**:
- Keep records of all income and expenses
- File Self Assessment tax return annually
- Pay Income Tax on profits

---

### Option 2: Limited Company (Recommended for Growth)
**Cost**: £50-300 (initial) + £240/year | **Time**: 1-2 weeks

**Pros**:
- Limited liability protection
- More professional
- Easier to raise investment
- Tax efficient

**Cons**:
- More paperwork
- Separate legal entity
- Accountancy costs

**How to Register**:
1. Go to Companies House website
2. Choose a company name (e.g., "Apex Leads Analytics Ltd")
3. Register online (£50) or paper (£71)
4. Get a business bank account
5. Register for Corporation Tax (within 3 months)

**Tax**:
- Corporation Tax on profits (currently 25% or 19% depending on profits)
- File annual accounts with Companies House
- File Confirmation Statement annually

---

## Recommended Path

### Phase 1: Start as Sole Trader (Month 1-6)
- Register as Sole Trader with HMRC
- Open a business bank account (even as sole trader)
- Start acquiring customers
- Validate the business model

### Phase 2: Incorporate as Ltd (Month 6-12)
Once you have:
- 5+ paying customers
- £1,000+ monthly revenue
- Plan to raise investment or scale

Then:
- Incorporate with Companies House
- Transfer assets to the company
- Get an accountant

## Step-by-Step Setup

### Step 1: Register as Sole Trader
1. Visit: www.gov.uk/register-for-self-assessment
2. Fill in the form
3. Get your Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR)
4. Register for VAT (optional, once you exceed £85,000)

### Step 2: Open Business Bank Account
**Recommended**:
- **Monzo Business** (free, easy)
- **Starling Business** (free, good features)
- **Barclays Business** (£8/month, traditional)

### Step 3: Set Up Accounting
**For Sole Trader**:
- Use FreeAgent (free with some banks) or Xero (£12/month)
- Track all income and expenses
- Save receipts

**For Ltd**:
- Hire an accountant (£300-600/year)
- Use Xero or QuickBooks (£12-30/month)

### Step 4: Get Insurance
**Recommended**:
- Professional Indemnity Insurance (£100-300/year)
- Public Liability Insurance (£100-200/year)
- Employers Liability Insurance (required if hiring employees)

**Providers**:
- Hiscox
- AXA
- Direct Line for Business

## What You Need Before Taking Payments

1. **Business Bank Account** - Required for Stripe payouts
2. **Registered Business** - Sole Trader or Ltd
3. **Tax Configuration** - VAT registration (if applicable)
4. **Terms of Service** - Legal protection for your platform
5. **Privacy Policy** - GDPR compliance

---

## Legal Documents

### Terms of Service
Should include:
- User registration terms
- Prohibited activities
- Limitation of liability
- Dispute resolution

**Options**:
- Use a free template (TermsFeed, Iubenda)
- Hire a solicitor (£200-500)

### Privacy Policy
Should include:
- What data you collect
- How you use data
- Data retention periods
- User rights

**Options**:
- Use a free template
- Generate with Iubenda (£10/month)

### Refund Policy
Should include:
- Cancellation rights
- Refund process
- Exceptions
